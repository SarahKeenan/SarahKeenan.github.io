Variable
 Description
case_ID ID code for each case of a pathogen infecting a deer
paper_ID ID code for each research paper included in this study
title Title of research paper
year Year of publication
month Month of publication
journal Scientific journal in which the paper was published
volume Journal volume number in which the paper was published
issue Journal issue number in which the paper was published
pages Page numbers of the paper within the issue/volume
authors Authors of the paper
language Language in which the paper was published
Country Country in which the deer samples were collected
Area_type Type of area the deer were sourced from (e.g. Game preserve, national park, hunting estate etc.)
Region The region of the study (as declared by the author) or the number of areas sampled if the study was country-wide
Location_type Either Local, Regional or Country-wide
Deer_species Species of deer studied (scientific name)
Deer_common Species of deer studied (common name)
Pathogen_type Type of pathogen studied in the deer (either bacterial, viral, protozoan, parasitic or fungal)
Pathogen_species Species of the pathogen studied in deer (scientific name)
Is_species_specified Whether the exact species (Yes) or just the larger pathogen group (No) was specified in the paper (e.g. Brucella sp. VS Brucella abortus)
Disease Disease caused in humans
Study_type Type of study described in paper (either prevalence, occurrence or case study)
No_samples_taken Number of deer samples taken to be tested
No_positive Number of deer samples that returned a positive result for the pathogen of interest
Prevalence (%) The calculated prevalence (%) of the pathogen within the group of deer tested (Either stated in the original paper or calculated the taking the number of positive samples as a percentage of the total samples)
No_samples_oubreak/occurrence In the case of papers covering occurrence/outbreak of a pathogen, this is the number of deer noted to have been infected with the pathogen
Detection_method Laboratory methods used to detect the presence of the pathogen
Sample_type The type of samples collected from deer used to test for the pathogen (e.g., serum, tissue, faecal etc.)
Evidence_of_pathogen Whether the pathogen was detected in deer samples using direct (e.g., genetic) or indirect methods (e.g., serological)
Evidence_of_transmission Whether the paper described or provided evidence of the transmission of the pathogen from deer to humans (yes/no)
Listed_transmission_types The modes of transmission of the zoonotic pathogen in a listed format
Predoninant_transmission_type The predominant mode of transmission of the zoonotic pathogen (eg, tickborne, foodborne, airborne, close contact, environmental etc.)
Insect-borne Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via insects (yes/no)
Food-borne Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via food (yes/no)
Air-borne Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via air (yes/no)
Water-borne Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via water (yes/no)
Environmental Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via environmental means (yes/no)
Close Contact Whether the pathogen can be transmitted via close contact (yes/no)
Transmission_direction The directions in which the pathogen can be transmitted; Anthropozoonosis = Animal to human transmission Amphixenosis = transmission is both ways
Listed_life_cycles Life cycle of the pathogen in a listed format
Direct Whether the pathogen has a direct life cycle and can be directly transmitted from animals to humans (yes/no)
Metazoonosis Whether the pathogen has a metazoonotic life cycle and requires both a vertebrate and an invertebrate host for completion of its life cycle (yes/no)
Cyclozoonosis Whether the pathogen has a cyclozoonotic life cycle and requires more than one vertebrate host for completion of the life cycle (yes/no)
Saprozoonosis Whether the pathogen has a saprozoonotic life cycle and requires both a vertebrate host and a nonanimal (food, soil, plant) reservoir for completion of its life cycle (yes/no)
Listed_habitat List of habitats that the reservoir hosts inhabit
Sylvatic A pathogen’s reservoir host inhabits primarily wild sylvatic environments (yes/no)
Peridomestic A pathogen’s reservoir host inhabits primarily areas around human habitations (yes/no)
Domestic A pathogen’s reservoir host inhabits primarily domestic environments (yes/no)
